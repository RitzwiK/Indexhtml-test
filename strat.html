Application strategy

Step 01
Target first-time orgs first
Orgs marked ★ have zero historical community bias. No established insiders. No mentor favorites. You start on equal footing with literally everyone.
Step 02
Use the domain gap as a weapon
Read one Wikipedia article about the org's field. Reference it in your first email to mentors. You instantly stand out from 90% of applicants who just say "I know Python."
Step 03
Make a PR before proposing
Even a docs fix or a failing test counts. A contributor with a merged PR gets accepted at 3–5× higher rates than a cold applicant with a perfect proposal.
Step 04
Email mentors directly
Find the mentor's email on the org's ideas page or GitHub. Ask one specific, thoughtful question about a project idea. Mentors who know your name favor your proposal.
Step 05
Submit 2 focused proposals
Maximum is 3. Submit to 2 orgs you've actually contributed to. Two focused proposals beat three rushed ones — mentors can tell the difference instantly.
Step 06
Write weekly milestones
Break your project into weekly deliverables with a clear timeline and a risk section. Mentors need to see you've thought it through, not just that you're enthusiastic.
